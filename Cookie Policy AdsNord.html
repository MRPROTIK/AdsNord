Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 2, 2026

For more information, please see our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies (such as pixels and tags) can also store or access information on your device.

2. Why we use cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Make the website work properly (essential functions)
  • Remember preferences (where enabled)
  • Measure and improve website performance (analytics)
  • Measure marketing performance and conversions (marketing), only when you consent

3. Types of cookies we use

Essential cookies (always on)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function (security, core features, and storing consent choices). They do not require consent under EU rules.

Analytics cookies (optional)

Used to understand how visitors use our website and improve performance. Activated only after you consent where required.

Marketing cookies (optional)

Used to measure ad performance, conversions, and in some cases support remarketing. Activated only after you consent where required.

Important: If you decline analytics/marketing cookies, we will not load those tags. Where applicable (e.g., Google Consent Mode), limited “consent signals/pings” may be sent to respect your choice while enabling aggregated measurement.

4. Tools we use on this website

5. How to manage your preferences

You can control cookies in these ways:

  • Use our cookie banner to Accept, Reject, or Customize cookies.
  • Change your browser settings to block or delete cookies.
  • Withdraw consent at any time via the button above or a “Cookie Settings” link in the footer (recommended).
Tip: If the button doesn’t open settings, your consent plugin may use a different method. The script below supports common platforms (Complianz, Cookiebot, Usercentrics, OneTrust). If you tell me which one you installed, I’ll tailor it 100%.

6. International data transfers

Some providers may process data outside the EU/EEA. Where international transfers occur, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and other legally recognized mechanisms, where applicable.

7. Updates to this Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.
